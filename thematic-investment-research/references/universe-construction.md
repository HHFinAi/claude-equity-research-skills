# Universe Construction Reference

## Universe Definition Principles

### Core Philosophy
- Capture genuine, material theme exposure
- Avoid over-inclusion (dilutes thematic purity)
- Avoid over-exclusion (misses legitimate beneficiaries)
- Balance pure-play concentration with diversified stability

## Inclusion Criteria Framework

### Revenue Exposure Thresholds

| Exposure Category | Revenue % | Strategic Focus | Portfolio Role |
|-------------------|-----------|-----------------|----------------|
| Pure-Play | >75% | Theme is core business | High conviction, concentrated |
| Significant | 25-75% | Theme is major segment | Core holdings |
| Enabler | 10-25% | Theme is material | Diversified exposure |
| Optionality | <10% current | Strategic initiatives | Speculative/watch list |

### Quantitative Screening Criteria

| Screen | Metric | Minimum Threshold | Rationale |
|--------|--------|-------------------|-----------|
| **Size** | Market Cap | $500M | Institutional investability |
| | Enterprise Value | $250M | Meaningful scale |
| **Liquidity** | Avg Daily Volume | $5M | Position sizing flexibility |
| | Free Float | >30% | Avoid ownership concentration |
| **Quality** | Gross Margin | >20% (sector adj) | Business model viability |
| | Net Debt/EBITDA | <4x | Financial sustainability |
| **Growth** | Revenue CAGR (3Y) | >5% | Above GDP growth |
| | Theme Revenue Growth | >15% | Thematic momentum |

### Qualitative Screening Factors

**Management Quality**
- [ ] Track record of execution
- [ ] Clear strategic vision aligned with theme
- [ ] Capital allocation discipline
- [ ] Insider ownership >2%
- [ ] Compensation tied to long-term metrics

**Technology/Competitive Position**
- [ ] Market leadership or clear differentiation
- [ ] IP/patent portfolio strength
- [ ] R&D productivity track record
- [ ] Technology on winning curve (not legacy)

**Customer Quality**
- [ ] High-quality customer base
- [ ] Contract visibility/backlog
- [ ] Low customer concentration (<25% top customer)
- [ ] Reference-able case studies

**ESG Considerations**
- [ ] Material ESG risks identified and managed
- [ ] No major controversies
- [ ] Governance meets minimum standards
- [ ] Alignment with theme's sustainability implications

## Exposure Categorization

### Pure-Play (>75% Thematic Revenue)

**Characteristics:**
- Highest beta to theme
- Focused management attention
- Clear investment thesis
- Often premium valuations

**Risks:**
- No diversification hedge
- Theme failure = position failure
- Often smaller, less liquid
- Higher volatility

**Portfolio Role:**
- Core conviction positions
- Size: 3-5% each
- Count: 3-5 positions

### Significant Exposure (25-75%)

**Characteristics:**
- Material theme leverage
- Some non-theme business cushion
- Often larger, more liquid
- More moderate valuations

**Benefits:**
- Better risk-adjusted returns
- Downside protection from diversification
- Often better liquidity
- Professional management resources

**Portfolio Role:**
- Anchor positions
- Size: 2-4% each
- Count: 5-8 positions

### Enabler (10-25%)

**Characteristics:**
- Indirect theme beneficiary
- Theme is meaningful but not dominant
- Often large-cap, liquid
- May be picks-and-shovels play

**Benefits:**
- Broad market exposure with theme tilt
- Lower volatility
- Deep liquidity
- Institutional-quality names

**Portfolio Role:**
- Diversified exposure
- Size: 1-2% each
- Count: 5-10 positions

### Optionality (<10% Current)

**Characteristics:**
- Early-stage theme exposure
- Strategic initiatives underway
- Management signaling pivot
- High uncertainty, high potential

**Use Cases:**
- Emerging theme exposure
- Optionality before pure-plays exist
- Potential takeout candidates

**Portfolio Role:**
- Speculative positions
- Size: 0.5-1% each
- Count: 3-5 positions

## Screening Process Workflow

### Step 1: Initial Universe Generation

**Sources for candidate identification:**
- Thematic ETF holdings
- Sell-side thematic research universes
- Industry association member lists
- Conference presenter/attendee lists
- Value chain mapping outputs
- Competitor mentions in filings

**Target**: 100-200 initial candidates

### Step 2: Liquidity Filter

Apply hard liquidity thresholds:
- Market cap >$500M
- ADV >$5M
- Free float >30%

**Expected attrition**: ~30%

### Step 3: Thematic Relevance Filter

Assess revenue exposure:
- Review segment disclosures
- Estimate theme-related revenue %
- Apply minimum 10% threshold

**Expected attrition**: ~30%

### Step 4: Quality Filter

Apply quality screens:
- Gross margin threshold
- Leverage threshold
- Profitability or path to profitability

**Expected attrition**: ~20%

### Step 5: Qualitative Review

Manual assessment of:
- Management quality
- Competitive positioning
- Technology alignment
- ESG considerations

**Expected attrition**: ~10%

### Final Universe
**Target**: 30-60 names across exposure categories

## Peer Group Construction

### Peer Group Types

**1. Value Chain Peers**
- Same layer in value chain
- Direct competitors
- Compare: market share, margins, growth

**2. Business Model Peers**
- Similar operating models
- May be different products
- Compare: unit economics, scalability

**3. Geographic Peers**
- Same regional exposure
- Similar regulatory environment
- Compare: market access, policy benefits

**4. Growth Stage Peers**
- Similar maturity level
- Comparable scale
- Compare: relative growth, valuation

## Universe Maintenance

### Refresh Triggers

**Quarterly:**
- Update financial metrics
- Review estimate revisions
- Assess relative positioning changes

**Semi-Annually:**
- Full universe screening refresh
- Add new IPOs/listings
- Reassess exposure categories
- Evaluate thesis progression

**Ad-Hoc:**
- Significant M&A activity
- Major strategy pivots
- Thesis-changing events
- Coverage initiation/drops

### Addition Criteria
- Passes all quantitative screens
- Demonstrates genuine thematic exposure
- Management commitment to theme
- Liquidity trajectory improving

### Removal Criteria
- Falls below liquidity thresholds
- Theme exposure diminishes
- Fundamental deterioration
- Thesis invalidation
- M&A takeout/delisting
