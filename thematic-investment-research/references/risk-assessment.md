## Risk Scoring Matrix

### Probability × Impact Framework

**Probability Scale:**
- 1: Remote (<10% chance)
- 2: Unlikely (10-30%)
- 3: Possible (30-50%)
- 4: Likely (50-70%)
- 5: Almost Certain (>70%)

**Impact Scale:**
- 1: Minimal (<5% value impact)
- 2: Minor (5-10% impact)
- 3: Moderate (10-20% impact)
- 4: Significant (20-40% impact)
- 5: Severe (>40% impact)

### Risk Heat Map

|  | Impact 1 | Impact 2 | Impact 3 | Impact 4 | Impact 5 |
|--|----------|----------|----------|----------|----------|
| **Prob 5** | 5 | 10 | 15 | 20 | 25 |
| **Prob 4** | 4 | 8 | 12 | 16 | 20 |
| **Prob 3** | 3 | 6 | 9 | 12 | 15 |
| **Prob 2** | 2 | 4 | 6 | 8 | 10 |
| **Prob 1** | 1 | 2 | 3 | 4 | 5 |

**Score Interpretation:**
- 1-4: **Monitor** - Track, no immediate action
- 5-9: **Plan** - Develop contingency plans
- 10-15: **Mitigate** - Active risk reduction
- 16-25: **Critical** - Reconsider position

## Thesis Killer Framework

### Definition
A thesis killer is a pre-defined condition that would invalidate the investment thesis and trigger position exit.

### Characteristics of Good Thesis Killers

| Characteristic | Description | Example |
|----------------|-------------|---------|
| **Specific** | Precisely defined | "Revenue declines 2 consecutive quarters" not "Revenue disappoints" |
| **Measurable** | Quantifiable | "Market share below 15%" not "Losing share" |
| **Observable** | Can be monitored | Data available quarterly or more frequently |
| **Pre-committed** | Defined before entry | Written in investment memo |
| **Action-triggering** | Clear response | "Exit 100%" not "Reassess" |

### Thesis Killer Categories

#### Theme-Level Killers

| Category | Example Thesis Killers |
|----------|----------------------|
| Technology | "Cost per kWh rises for 2 consecutive quarters" |
| Adoption | "Penetration rate falls below 5% by 2026" |
| Regulation | "Key subsidy expires without renewal" |
| Competition | "Market leader enters with 50% cost advantage" |

#### Company-Level Killers

| Category | Example Thesis Killers |
|----------|----------------------|
| Market Position | "Market share falls below 15% in core segment" |
| Financial | "Net debt/EBITDA exceeds 4.0x" |
| Execution | "Miss revenue guidance 3 consecutive quarters" |
| Management | "CEO and CFO both depart within 12 months" |
| Technology | "Key product loses certification/approval" |

#### Valuation-Level Killers

| Category | Example Thesis Killers |
|----------|----------------------|
| Multiple | "Stock trades above 50x forward P/E for 6+ months" |
| Reverse DCF | "Implied growth exceeds 2x TAM CAGR" |

## Risk Mitigation Strategies

### Portfolio-Level Mitigation

| Strategy | Application | Trade-offs |
|----------|-------------|------------|
| **Diversification** | Multiple positions across value chain | Dilutes alpha; monitoring burden |
| **Position Sizing** | Inverse to risk score | Limits upside in high-conviction |
| **Correlation Management** | Limit correlated exposures | May exclude good opportunities |

### Position-Level Mitigation

| Strategy | Application | Trade-offs |
|----------|-------------|------------|
| **Stop Losses** | Price-based exit triggers | Whipsaw risk; may exit prematurely |
| **Fundamental Stops** | Thesis killer framework | Lagging; requires monitoring |
| **Options Hedging** | Protective puts, collars | Cost of carry; basis risk |
| **Pair Trades** | Long winner / short loser | Additional margin; execution risk |

### Staged Entry for Risk Management

| Stage | Size | Trigger | Purpose |
|-------|------|---------|---------|
| Initial | 40-50% | Thesis validated | Establish core position |
| Second | 30-40% | Positive confirmation | Build on conviction |
| Final | 10-30% | Clear catalyst | Complete position |

### Risk-Adjusted Position Sizing

**Base Size Adjustments:**

| Factor | Adjustment |
|--------|------------|
| High volatility (>2x market) | -25% to -50% |
| Low liquidity (<$10M ADV) | -25% to -50% |
| High correlation to existing | -25% |
| Near thesis killer threshold | -50% |
| Elevated crowding indicators | -25% |
