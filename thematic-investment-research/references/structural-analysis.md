# Structural Analysis Reference

## Market Sizing Methodology

### Top-Down Approach

**Step 1: Define the broadest relevant market**
- Start with macro-level data (GDP, sector output, end-market size)
- Source from government statistics, multilateral organizations

**Step 2: Apply segmentation**
- Geographic breakdown
- Customer segment breakdown
- Product/service category breakdown

**Step 3: Apply penetration assumptions**
- Current penetration rate
- Projected penetration trajectory
- Penetration ceiling (rarely 100%)

**Step 4: Apply growth rates**
- GDP growth baseline
- Segment-specific growth premiums
- Technology-driven acceleration factors

**Step 5: Cross-reference**
- Compare to published industry forecasts (Gartner, IDC, IHS)
- Identify and explain variances

### Bottom-Up Approach

**Step 1: Identify customer segments**
- List all potential customer types
- Estimate number of potential customers per segment

**Step 2: Estimate unit economics**
- Average revenue per customer
- Purchase frequency
- Contract value / deal size

**Step 3: Apply realistic adoption**
- Early adopter penetration (typically 10-15%)
- Early majority (next 35%)
- Late majority (next 35%)
- Laggards (final 15%)

**Step 4: Build timeline**
- Year-by-year adoption curve
- Apply S-curve adoption model where appropriate

**Step 5: Reconcile with top-down**
- Compare totals
- Investigate material differences
- Document assumption differences

### Common Market Sizing Errors

| Error | Correction |
|-------|------------|
| Assuming 100% TAM capture | Apply realistic serviceable market |
| Ignoring cannibalization | Account for substitution effects |
| Linear growth assumption | Use S-curve for technology adoption |
| Single-source TAM | Triangulate 3+ independent estimates |
| Ignoring price deflation | Model learning curve effects |

## Value Chain Mapping Framework

### Value Chain Layer Definitions

**Upstream Inputs**
- Raw materials and components
- Foundational technologies
- IP/Patent licensors
- R&D and innovation sources

**Midstream Enablers**
- Manufacturing and assembly
- System integration
- Platform providers
- Infrastructure operators

**Downstream Delivery**
- End-product manufacturers
- Service providers
- Distribution and logistics
- Installation and maintenance

**Adjacent Beneficiaries**
- Complementary products
- Professional services
- Financing providers
- Secondary market participants

### Layer-by-Layer Analysis Questions

**For Each Layer, Document:**

1. **Market Structure**
   - Number of competitors
   - Market share concentration (HHI)
   - Geographic distribution
   - Vertical integration prevalence

2. **Economics**
   - Gross margin range
   - Operating margin range
   - ROIC benchmarks
   - Working capital requirements

3. **Competitive Dynamics**
   - Basis of competition (cost, differentiation, innovation)
   - Switching costs
   - Network effects
   - Scale advantages

4. **Growth Characteristics**
   - Volume vs. price-driven growth
   - Cyclicality
   - Regulatory sensitivity
   - Technology evolution risk

5. **Investment Implications**
   - Preferred exposure point
   - Risk/reward profile
   - Liquidity of investable names
   - Pure-play availability
