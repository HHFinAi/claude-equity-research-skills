# Theme Validation Reference

## DRIVER Framework Deep Dive

### D - Durability

**Definition**: Multi-year persistence of the underlying driver

**Validation Questions**:
- Is this driven by structural forces (demographics, physics, regulation) or temporary factors?
- What is the minimum realistic time horizon for this theme?
- Has this theme persisted through previous cycles?
- What could accelerate or decelerate the driver?

**Evidence Types**:
- Demographic trends with 10+ year projections
- Technology cost curves with historical learning rates
- Regulatory mandates with implementation timelines
- Infrastructure buildout requirements

**Red Flags**:
- Heavily dependent on single policy/subsidy
- Technology still in lab stage
- Consumer preference-driven without structural lock-in

### R - Relevance

**Definition**: Material impact on corporate fundamentals

**Validation Questions**:
- Does this theme drive revenue growth, margin expansion, or multiple re-rating?
- Is the impact quantifiable and attributable?
- Does it affect core business or peripheral segments?
- Is the impact large enough to move the needle on enterprise value?

**Evidence Types**:
- Management commentary on strategic priority
- Capex allocation toward theme
- Revenue contribution trending upward
- Margin expansion linked to theme execution

**Materiality Thresholds**:
- Revenue: >10% of company revenue should be theme-related
- Growth: Theme-related growth should be >50% above base growth
- Margin: Theme should contribute to margin improvement

### I - Investability

**Definition**: Availability of adequate investment vehicles

**Validation Questions**:
- Are there listed equities with genuine exposure?
- Is liquidity sufficient for meaningful position sizing?
- Are there pure-play options or only conglomerates?
- What is the market cap spectrum of investable names?

**Liquidity Thresholds**:
- Minimum average daily volume: $5M
- Minimum market cap: $500M for institutional
- Float: >30% for meaningful institutional ownership

**Vehicle Types**:
- Direct equities (preferred)
- ETFs (for diversified exposure)
- ADRs for international exposure
- Private markets (for longer horizons, illiquid)

### V - Verifiability

**Definition**: Thesis elements can be empirically tested

**Validation Questions**:
- What data sources confirm or refute the thesis?
- What leading indicators would signal thesis progression or failure?
- How frequently is confirming data available?
- Are there independent sources for validation?

**Leading Indicators by Theme Type**:

| Theme Type | Leading Indicators |
|------------|-------------------|
| Technology Adoption | Patent filings, VC funding, developer activity |
| Consumer Behavior | Search trends, app downloads, survey data |
| Industrial/Infrastructure | Permits, order books, capacity announcements |
| Regulatory-Driven | Legislative calendar, regulatory comments |
| Demographic | Census data, labor statistics, healthcare utilization |

**Data Frequency Requirements**:
- High-frequency (weekly/monthly): Tactical themes
- Medium-frequency (quarterly): Structural themes
- Low-frequency (annually): Mega themes

### E - Expansion

**Definition**: Addressable market growing in absolute terms

**Validation Questions**:
- Is the TAM expanding through new use cases or geographies?
- What is the realistic market size trajectory?
- Are there adjacent markets that could be captured?
- What is the ceiling on penetration?

**Market Sizing Validation**:
- Compare 3+ independent TAM estimates
- Build bottom-up model from unit economics
- Apply realistic penetration ceiling (not 100%)
- Discount for competitive intensity

**Growth Decomposition**:
- Volume growth: New customers, increased usage
- Price growth: Mix shift, pricing power
- Geographic expansion: New markets
- Adjacency expansion: New use cases

### R - Rationality

**Definition**: Current pricing does not fully discount opportunity

**Validation Questions**:
- What level of theme success is embedded in valuations?
- Is there skepticism or under-appreciation to exploit?
- How do current multiples compare to historical range?
- What growth is implied by current prices?

**Reverse DCF Analysis**:
1. Take current market prices
2. Assume reasonable discount rate and terminal growth
3. Calculate implied revenue/earnings CAGR
4. Compare to consensus and your own estimates

**Crowding Indicators**:
- ETF AUM growth: >50% YoY suggests crowding
- Analyst coverage expansion: Broad coverage = consensus
- Conference mentions: Everyone discussing = priced in
- Multiple expansion: Near historical highs = caution

## Gartner Hype Cycle Assessment

### Stage Identification

| Stage | Characteristics | Investment Implications |
|-------|----------------|------------------------|
| Innovation Trigger | Early R&D, limited commercial viability | VC appropriate; public equity very speculative |
| Peak of Expectations | Media frenzy, first-gen products | Valuations likely stretched; timing risk high |
| Trough of Disillusionment | Failed expectations, consolidation | Value opportunity; survivor identification critical |
| Slope of Enlightenment | Use cases clarify, business models prove | Optimal entry for public equity |
| Plateau of Productivity | Mainstream adoption | Theme may be mature; focus on market leaders |

### Stage Assessment Checklist

**Innovation Trigger Indicators**:
- [ ] Technology primarily in academic papers
- [ ] VC funding but limited revenue
- [ ] < 5 public companies with exposure
- [ ] No established business models

**Peak of Expectations Indicators**:
- [ ] Heavy media coverage
- [ ] Multiple IPOs/SPACs in space
- [ ] Sell-side coverage expanding rapidly
- [ ] Premium valuations across the sector

**Trough Indicators**:
- [ ] Failed companies/bankruptcies
- [ ] Analyst downgrades widespread
- [ ] Valuations compressed 50%+ from peak
- [ ] Funding difficult for private companies

**Slope of Enlightenment Indicators**:
- [ ] Clear use case winners emerging
- [ ] Profitability for leading players
- [ ] Customer case studies available
- [ ] Consolidation stabilizing competitive dynamics

**Plateau Indicators**:
- [ ] Theme well-understood by market
- [ ] Mature business models
- [ ] Competition primarily on cost/scale
- [ ] Growth converging to GDP+

## Theme Classification Matrix

### By Duration

| Category | Duration | Characteristics | Examples |
|----------|----------|-----------------|----------|
| Mega | 10-30 years | Economy-wide; diffuse exposure | AI, Demographics, Climate |
| Structural | 5-15 years | Sector-transformative; pure-plays exist | EVs, Precision Medicine, Cloud |
| Cyclical | 2-7 years | Policy/cycle-driven; timing-sensitive | Infrastructure Spending, Reshoring |
| Tactical | 6-24 months | Event-driven; high turnover | Election trades, Post-crisis recovery |

### By Driver Type

| Driver | Predictability | Thesis Stability | Examples |
|--------|---------------|------------------|----------|
| Technology | Medium | High (cost curves) | Batteries, Solar, Computing |
| Demographics | High | Very High | Aging, Urbanization |
| Regulation | Low | Medium (policy risk) | Carbon pricing, Healthcare reform |
| Behavioral | Low | Low | Consumer preferences |
