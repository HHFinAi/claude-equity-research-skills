# Competitive Analysis Framework - Detailed Methodologies

This reference provides detailed analytical methodologies for all 10 competitive dimensions.

## Section 1: Market Structure & Positioning

**Objective**: Assess the company's position within industry competitive landscape.

**Key metrics to calculate:**
- Market share (absolute %, rank, trend over 3-5 years)
- Industry concentration: Herfindahl-Hirschman Index (HHI = Σ(market share)²)
- Concentration Ratio (CR4 = sum of top 4 players' market shares)
- Relative market share (company share / largest competitor share)

**Analysis questions:**
- Industry structure: Fragmented (<40% CR4), moderately concentrated (40-70% CR4), highly concentrated (>70% CR4)?
- Growth stage: Emerging, rapid growth, mature, declining?
- Company trajectory: Gaining/losing share? Rate of change?
- Positioning: Market leader, strong #2, challenger, niche specialist?

**Data sources**: Industry reports (Gartner, IDC, Forrester), company filings, trade associations

**Output format:**
- Market share table with 5-year trend
- Competitive landscape map (positioning matrix)
- Industry life cycle assessment
- Trend narrative (1-2 paragraphs)

## Section 2: Competitive Moat Assessment

**Objective**: Evaluate sources and sustainability of competitive advantages using Morningstar moat framework.

**Five moat sources to evaluate:**

### 2.1 Intangible Assets
- **Patents**: Count, quality, expiration timeline, litigation history
- **Brands**: Brand value estimates, awareness metrics, pricing premium vs. generic
- **Regulatory licenses**: Exclusivity period, barriers to new licenses
- **Proprietary data**: Scale, uniqueness, network effects from data

**Rating scale**: None (0-2), Narrow (3-4), Wide (5)

### 2.2 Switching Costs
- Customer switching costs: Integration depth, training investment, data migration complexity
- Examples: ERP systems (high), consumer apps (low)
- Quantify: Switching cost as % of annual spend, average customer tenure

### 2.3 Network Effects
- **Direct**: Value grows with users (e.g., Facebook, payment networks)
- **Indirect**: Two-sided platforms (e.g., Uber, app stores)
- **Data network**: More users → more data → better product
- Measure: % market share needed for network effect lock-in

### 2.4 Cost Advantages
- **Scale economies**: Unit cost decline with volume, minimum efficient scale
- **Unique assets**: Favorable locations, raw material access, proprietary technology
- **Process advantages**: Manufacturing know-how, operational efficiency
- Benchmark: Cost per unit vs. #2 and #3 competitors

### 2.5 Efficient Scale
- Natural monopoly characteristics: Infrastructure industries, local utilities
- Evidence: Stable market shares, high barriers to entry, limited new entrants

**Moat rating methodology:**
- **Wide Moat**: 15+ years of competitive advantage expected, multiple strong moat sources, high barriers to erosion
- **Narrow Moat**: 10 years of competitive advantage expected, 1-2 moat sources, some vulnerability to disruption
- **No Moat**: <5 years of sustainable advantage, easily replicable, commoditized

**Output format:**
- Moat rating with duration estimate
- Strength assessment for each moat source (0-5 scale)
- Moat trend: Widening, stable, narrowing
- Key supporting evidence (3-5 bullet points per source)

## Section 3: Porter's Five Forces Analysis

**Objective**: Assess industry profitability and structural attractiveness.

### 3.1 Threat of New Entrants
**Barriers to entry assessment:**
- Capital requirements ($ needed to compete effectively)
- Economies of scale (cost disadvantage for small entrants)
- Product differentiation (brand loyalty, switching costs)
- Access to distribution channels
- Regulatory barriers (licenses, patents, approvals)
- Incumbent retaliation risk

**Rating**: Low (1-2), Medium (3), High (4-5) barrier strength

### 3.2 Bargaining Power of Suppliers
- Supplier concentration vs. industry concentration
- Switching costs to alternative suppliers
- Availability of substitutes for suppliers' products
- Importance of industry to supplier (% of their revenue)
- Forward integration threat

**Assessment**: Calculate weighted supplier power score (1-5)

### 3.3 Bargaining Power of Customers
- Customer concentration vs. seller concentration
- Volume purchased as % of seller's revenue
- Switching costs for customers
- Product differentiation (commoditized vs. differentiated)
- Price sensitivity (discretionary vs. essential)
- Backward integration threat

### 3.4 Threat of Substitutes
- Relative price-performance of substitutes
- Switching costs to substitutes
- Customer propensity to substitute
- Examples: Video conferencing (substitute for business travel), streaming (substitute for cable)

### 3.5 Intensity of Competitive Rivalry
- Number and balance of competitors
- Industry growth rate
- Fixed costs and exit barriers
- Product differentiation
- Strategic stakes (importance to competitors)

**Scoring methodology:**
Each force: 1 (favorable) to 5 (unfavorable)
Average = industry attractiveness score

**Output format:**
- Five Forces spider chart (1-5 scale)
- Industry attractiveness score with interpretation
- Key dynamics paragraph for each force
- Overall implication: Favorable/neutral/unfavorable for profitability

## Section 4: Value Chain & Cost Position

**Objective**: Analyze activities that create value and assess cost competitiveness.

### 4.1 Value Chain Mapping
**Primary activities:**
- Inbound logistics: Raw material sourcing, inventory management
- Operations: Manufacturing, assembly, quality control
- Outbound logistics: Warehousing, distribution, delivery
- Marketing & sales: Advertising, channel management, sales force
- Service: Installation, training, support, warranty

**Support activities:**
- Firm infrastructure: Management, finance, legal
- HR management: Recruiting, training, compensation
- Technology development: R&D, process improvement, IT systems
- Procurement: Purchasing, vendor management

**Analysis**: For each activity, assess:
- Cost as % of total cost structure
- Relative performance vs. competitors (better/same/worse)
- Opportunities for differentiation or cost advantage

### 4.2 Cost Position Benchmarking
**Key metrics:**
- Gross margin trend (5 years)
- Operating margin vs. peers (percentile ranking)
- SG&A as % of revenue vs. peers
- R&D efficiency (revenue per R&D dollar, or patents per R&D dollar)

**Cost structure analysis:**
- Fixed vs. variable costs
- Operating leverage (% change in EBIT for 1% revenue change)
- Economies of scale evidence

### 4.3 Pricing Power Assessment
- Frequency of price increases
- Price increase realization (% passed to customers)
- Premium vs. competitors (% price differential)
- Volume retention after price increases

**Output format:**
- Value chain diagram with competitive assessment
- Cost structure breakdown vs. peers
- Margin trend charts with peer comparison
- Pricing power narrative with evidence

## Section 5: Strategic Positioning

**Objective**: Evaluate strategic coherence and competitive strategy effectiveness.

### 5.1 Generic Strategy Classification
**Porter's Generic Strategies:**
- **Cost Leadership**: Lowest cost producer, targets broad market
- **Differentiation**: Unique product/service, premium pricing, targets broad market
- **Focus (Cost)**: Lowest cost in narrow segment
- **Focus (Differentiation)**: Unique offering in narrow segment

**Assessment criteria:**
- Strategy clarity and consistency
- Alignment between value proposition and cost structure
- Evidence of successful execution (margins, market share)

### 5.2 Strategic Coherence
- Consistency between stated strategy and resource allocation
- Alignment of org structure, incentives, and strategy
- Capital allocation decisions support strategic priorities?

### 5.3 Competitive Response Patterns
- Historical response to competitive threats (pricing, product, marketing)
- Response speed and effectiveness
- Predictability of responses

### 5.4 Strategic Flexibility
- Optionality in strategy (multiple paths to value creation)
- Asset fungibility (can redeploy assets to different uses)
- Exit barriers (sunk costs, contractual obligations)

**Output format:**
- Strategy classification with supporting evidence
- Coherence assessment (1-5 scale)
- Track record of strategic execution
- Strategic options analysis

## Section 6: Innovation & Adaptability

**Objective**: Assess ability to innovate and adapt to change.

### 6.1 R&D Efficiency
**Metrics:**
- R&D spend as % of revenue vs. peers
- Revenue from new products (<3 years old)
- Patent productivity: Patents granted / R&D spend
- Time to market vs. competitors
- R&D ROI: NPV of new products / R&D investment

### 6.2 Innovation Track Record
- New product success rate (% that meet targets)
- Market share in new categories entered
- Examples of successful innovation vs. failures
- Speed of innovation adoption

### 6.3 Technology Position
- Technology generation vs. competitors (leading/keeping pace/lagging)
- Platform vs. point solution (scalability)
- Proprietary vs. licensed technology
- Technology roadmap visibility

### 6.4 Digital Transformation Progress
- Digital revenue as % of total
- Cloud/SaaS transition progress
- Data analytics capabilities (descriptive/predictive/prescriptive)
- AI/ML integration in products/operations

### 6.5 Organizational Adaptability
- Speed of decision-making
- Risk tolerance and experimentation culture
- M&A integration capabilities
- Response to past disruptions

**Output format:**
- R&D efficiency scorecard
- Innovation pipeline assessment
- Technology adoption S-curve positioning
- Digital maturity assessment

## Section 7: Operational Competitiveness

**Objective**: Evaluate operational efficiency and execution quality.

### 7.1 Asset Efficiency
**Metrics:**
- Asset turnover (Revenue / Total assets)
- Fixed asset turnover (Revenue / PP&E)
- Inventory turnover (COGS / Average inventory)
- Days sales outstanding (DSO)
- Cash conversion cycle (DIO + DSO - DPO)

**Benchmarking**: Percentile ranking vs. industry peers

### 7.2 Working Capital Management
- Working capital as % of revenue (trend)
- Working capital efficiency vs. competitors
- Cash flow conversion (CFO / Net income)

### 7.3 Quality & Reliability
- Product defect rates
- Warranty expense as % of revenue
- Customer satisfaction scores (NPS, CSAT)
- Service uptime / reliability metrics

### 7.4 Supply Chain Resilience
- Supplier diversification (concentration risk)
- Geographic diversity of supply chain
- Inventory buffers and safety stock
- Response to past supply disruptions

### 7.5 Manufacturing / Operations Excellence
- Capacity utilization trends
- Manufacturing cycle time
- Yield rates / scrap rates
- Lean implementation (if applicable)

**Output format:**
- Asset efficiency dashboard with peer comparison
- Working capital trend analysis
- Quality metrics vs. standards
- Supply chain risk heat map

## Section 8: Financial Competitiveness Metrics

**Objective**: Benchmark financial performance and value creation.

### 8.1 Return Metrics
**Key ratios:**
- ROIC (NOPAT / Invested Capital) vs. WACC
- ROE vs. cost of equity
- ROA vs. peers
- Trend analysis (5-10 years)

**Value creation test**: ROIC - WACC = economic profit margin
- Positive spread = value creation
- Negative spread = value destruction

### 8.2 Profitability Analysis
**Margin progression:**
- Gross margin trend and peer rank
- Operating margin (EBITDA, EBIT) trend and peer rank
- Net margin trend and peer rank

**DuPont analysis**: ROE = Net margin × Asset turnover × Equity multiplier
- Decompose to understand drivers of returns

### 8.3 Cash Generation
**Metrics:**
- Free cash flow (FCF) margin = FCF / Revenue
- Cash conversion rate = CFO / EBITDA
- FCF vs. net income (quality of earnings)
- Capital intensity = Capex / Revenue

### 8.4 Financial Strength
- Leverage ratios: Net Debt/EBITDA, Debt/Equity
- Interest coverage ratio (EBIT / Interest expense)
- Credit rating and trajectory
- Financial flexibility for growth investments

**Output format:**
- ROIC decomposition table (margin × turnover)
- Margin progression chart (5-year trend vs. peers)
- Cash generation quality assessment
- Financial strength scorecard

## Section 9: Vulnerability Analysis

**Objective**: Identify and assess key risks to competitive position.

### 9.1 Concentration Risks
**Customer concentration:**
- Top 10 customers as % of revenue
- Single customer > 10% of revenue? (disclosure threshold)
- Risk assessment: Low (<20%), Medium (20-40%), High (>40%)

**Supplier concentration:**
- Single-source suppliers for critical inputs
- Geographic concentration of supply chain
- Alternative suppliers available?

**Product concentration:**
- Top product/segment as % of revenue
- Patent cliff or product lifecycle risk
- Pipeline to offset declines?

### 9.2 Regulatory & Political Risks
- Regulatory approval requirements
- Compliance costs as % of revenue
- Pending regulatory changes (identify and assess)
- Political exposure (tariffs, trade policy, government contracts)
- Antitrust risk (market share thresholds)

### 9.3 Technology Disruption Risks
- Substitute technologies emerging?
- Business model disruption threats (e.g., SaaS vs. perpetual license)
- Technology adoption cycle stage
- Investment in next-gen technology vs. peers

### 9.4 ESG & Reputational Risks
- Carbon intensity vs. peers (relevant for high-emission industries)
- Labor practices and controversies
- Data privacy and cybersecurity incidents
- Social media sentiment and brand reputation

### 9.5 Execution Risks
- Management turnover (key person risk)
- M&A integration challenges
- Geographic expansion execution
- New product launch track record

**Risk quantification methodology:**
- Probability: Low (1), Medium (2), High (3)
- Impact: Low (1), Medium (2), High (3)
- Risk score = Probability × Impact (1-9 scale)

**Output format:**
- Concentration risk dashboard (customer, supplier, product)
- Regulatory risk matrix with probability/impact scoring
- Technology disruption threat assessment
- Top 5 vulnerabilities ranked by risk score

## Section 10: Forward-Looking Indicators

**Objective**: Identify leading indicators of competitive trajectory.

### 10.1 Market Share in Emerging Segments
- Position in fastest-growing product categories
- Share gains/losses in new markets
- Strategic initiatives in high-growth areas
- Investment allocation to future businesses

### 10.2 Competitive Win/Loss Rates
- Win rate trends in competitive bids (if disclosed or observable)
- Head-to-head performance vs. key competitors
- Pricing discipline in competitive situations
- Customer churn rate trends

### 10.3 Customer Satisfaction & Loyalty
**Metrics:**
- Net Promoter Score (NPS) trend
- Customer satisfaction (CSAT) scores
- Customer retention rate
- Upsell/cross-sell success rates
- Customer lifetime value trends

### 10.4 Employee Metrics
- Glassdoor rating and trend
- Employee turnover rate vs. industry
- Recruiting success (offer acceptance rate, time-to-fill)
- Internal promotion rate (talent development)
- Engineering talent concentration (LinkedIn analysis)

### 10.5 Innovation Pipeline Indicators
- Pipeline visibility (disclosed future products)
- Patent filing trends (volume and quality)
- R&D project milestones
- Clinical trial success rates (for biotech/pharma)
- Beta customer adoption of new products

### 10.6 Strategic Investment Signals
- M&A activity in adjacent markets
- Venture investments / corporate venture capital activity
- Partnership announcements with leading players
- Geographic expansion moves
- Capacity expansion or rationalization

### 10.7 Management Commentary & Tone
- Confidence level in guidance and outlook
- Strategic clarity and consistency
- Competitive positioning claims
- Response to competitor actions

**Output format:**
- Leading indicators dashboard (traffic light: green/yellow/red)
- Trend table (last 8 quarters if available)
- Narrative synthesis of forward trajectory
- Red flags and positive signals summary

## Section 11: Geopolitical Supply Chain Resilience

**Objective**: Assess supply chain exposure to geopolitical risk and evaluate domestic/friend-shored positioning as a competitive advantage.

### 11.1 Supply Chain Geographic Exposure Mapping

**Tier analysis:**
- **Tier 1 suppliers**: Direct supplier country concentration (% by country/region)
- **Tier 2+ suppliers**: Critical sub-supplier geographic dependencies
- **Manufacturing footprint**: Own facilities by country/region
- **Revenue exposure**: Revenue by end-market geography

**Key metrics:**
- China/Taiwan exposure (% of supply chain value)
- Allied nation concentration (% from NATO/AUKUS/Quad members)
- Nearshored concentration (% from same region as HQ)
- Single-country dependency risk (any country >30% of critical inputs)

**Country risk classification:**
| Category | Examples | Risk Level |
|----------|----------|------------|
| Allied/Stable | US, EU, Japan, Australia, Canada | Low |
| Aligned/Developing | India, Mexico, Vietnam, Poland | Low-Medium |
| Neutral/Uncertain | Brazil, Indonesia, Turkey, Saudi Arabia | Medium |
| Strategic Concern | China, Taiwan, Russia | High |
| Conflict Zone | Ukraine, Middle East | Very High |

### 11.2 Friend-shored/Nearshored Advantage Assessment

**Competitive advantage indicators:**
- Domestic manufacturing as % of COGS (vs. industry average)
- Nearshored capacity as % of total (within same continent)
- Friend-shored capacity as % of total (allied nations)
- Supply chain transformation progress (reshoring investments, timelines)

**Comparative positioning:**
- Relative supply chain security vs. top 3 competitors
- First-mover advantage in reshoring (years ahead of competitors)
- Government incentive capture (IRA, CHIPS Act, EU subsidies)
- Customer preference for supply chain security (contract wins, pricing power)

**Quantification framework:**
- Cost premium absorbed for domestic/ally sourcing (% COGS impact)
- Lead time advantage vs. offshore competitors (days/weeks)
- Inventory carrying cost savings from shorter supply chains
- Carbon footprint advantage from reduced shipping

### 11.3 Geopolitical Risk Scoring

**Country-level risk index (1-10 scale):**
Composite of four inputs per BCG framework:
1. **Trade & business climate** (tariff levels, trade agreement coverage, currency stability)
2. **National security & foreign relations** (alliance status, sanction risk, military tensions)
3. **Internal political stability** (regime stability, rule of law, corruption)
4. **Regulatory & export control risk** (technology transfer restrictions, data localization, forced JV requirements)

**Product-level risk assessment:**
- Critical material dependency (rare earths, chips, pharmaceuticals, batteries)
- Dual-use technology exposure (export control vulnerability)
- Data sovereignty requirements (cloud, AI, telecommunications)
- Strategic industry classification (defense, energy, healthcare)

**Aggregated supply chain geopolitical risk score:**
- Weight country risk by % of supply chain value from each country
- Multiply by product criticality factor (1-3x for strategic items)
- Resulting score: Low (<3), Medium (3-5), High (5-7), Critical (>7)

### 11.4 Critical Material & Component Dependency

**Chokepoint analysis:**
- Identify components with >50% global production in high-risk geographies
- Map critical minerals exposure (lithium, cobalt, rare earths, gallium, germanium)
- Assess semiconductor dependency (leading-edge vs. mature nodes, foundry concentration)
- Pharmaceutical API exposure (China/India concentration)

**Alternative source assessment:**
- Availability of qualified alternative suppliers
- Time to qualify new suppliers (months/years)
- Cost differential for alternative sources
- Capacity constraints in friendly nations

**Strategic stockpile & buffer analysis:**
- Inventory policy vs. just-in-time peers
- Safety stock in weeks of demand for critical items
- Strategic reserve arrangements (government or private)
- Contractual supply guarantees and take-or-pay commitments

### 11.5 Tariff & Trade Policy Vulnerability

**Current tariff exposure:**
- Effective tariff rate on imports (by origin country)
- Effective tariff rate on exports (by destination)
- Section 301/232 exposure (US)
- CBAM exposure (EU carbon border adjustment)

**Scenario analysis:**
| Scenario | Probability | Revenue Impact | Margin Impact |
|----------|-------------|----------------|---------------|
| Status quo | X% | 0% | 0% |
| Moderate escalation (25% tariff increase) | X% | -X% | -X bps |
| Severe escalation (50%+ tariffs, export controls) | X% | -X% | -X bps |
| Trade war / decoupling | X% | -X% | -X bps |

**Trade agreement positioning:**
- USMCA coverage (% of North American trade)
- EU FTA coverage (% of European trade)
- RCEP/CPTPP coverage (% of Asia-Pacific trade)
- Bilateral agreement advantages vs. competitors

### 11.6 Time-to-Survive & Time-to-Recover Metrics

**Time-to-Survive (TTS):**
- Days of inventory for critical components if supply disrupted
- Alternative supplier activation timeline (days to first delivery)
- Contractual supply guarantees (volume and duration)
- Cash runway for elevated input costs

**Time-to-Recover (TTR):**
- Time to qualify and scale alternative suppliers (weeks/months)
- Capacity ramp timeline in friendly geographies
- Capital investment required for supply chain restructuring
- Regulatory approval timelines for new sources (especially pharma, defense)

**Resilience gap analysis:**
- TTR > TTS = vulnerability (need buffer investment)
- TTR ≤ TTS = adequate resilience
- Quantify buffer investment required to close gaps

### 11.7 Reshoring/Nearshoring Optionality Value

**Transformation in progress:**
- Announced reshoring investments ($ and timeline)
- Nearshoring capacity additions (% of total capacity)
- Friend-shoring partnerships (JVs, long-term contracts in allied nations)
- Vertical integration moves (bringing critical processes in-house)

**Optionality assessment:**
- Asset flexibility (can facilities be repurposed for different products?)
- Contract flexibility (break clauses, volume flexibility with suppliers)
- Capital available for supply chain transformation
- Management bandwidth and execution capability

**Government support capture:**
- CHIPS Act grants/loans secured or pending
- IRA manufacturing credits eligibility
- State/local incentives for domestic manufacturing
- Defense Production Act priority access

### 11.8 Investment Implications

**Valuation multiple impact:**
- Premium justified for supply chain resilience? (Estimate: +X% EV/EBITDA)
- Peer discount for concentrated China exposure
- Multiple expansion potential from reshoring execution

**Earnings risk adjustment:**
- Probability-weighted EPS impact from trade scenarios
- Margin sustainability under elevated tariff environment
- Working capital implications of higher safety stock

**Competitive positioning dynamics:**
- Market share opportunity if competitors face disruption
- Pricing power from supply security (customer willingness to pay premium)
- Contract wins attributed to supply chain positioning

**Red flags:**
- >50% of supply chain value from high-risk geographies with no transformation plan
- Critical single-source dependencies in conflict-risk regions
- Competitor significantly ahead in reshoring/friend-shoring
- Management dismissive of geopolitical risk in commentary

**Green flags:**
- Proactive reshoring investments before government mandates
- Diversified supplier base across allied nations
- Contractual supply security with tier 2+ visibility
- Demonstrated supply chain agility in past disruptions

**Output format:**
- Geographic exposure heat map (by country and tier)
- Geopolitical risk scorecard (1-10 across sub-dimensions)
- TTS vs. TTR gap analysis table
- Trade scenario sensitivity analysis
- Competitive benchmarking vs. peers on supply chain resilience
- Investment implications summary with valuation impact estimate

## Analytical Best Practices

### Data Collection Priorities
1. **Primary sources first**: Company filings (10-K, 10-Q, proxy), transcripts, investor presentations
2. **Industry sources**: Trade associations, market research (Gartner, IDC, Forrester, McKinsey)
3. **Third-party validation**: Customer reviews, Glassdoor, patent databases, news articles
4. **Competitor benchmarking**: Peer company filings for comparative metrics

### Objectivity Checklist
- Did you identify both strengths AND weaknesses?
- Did you quantify claims with specific metrics?
- Did you benchmark against relevant peers?
- Did you avoid confirmation bias (seeking only supportive evidence)?
- Did you consider alternative explanations?

### Investment Implications Framework
For each section, connect findings to:
- **Valuation**: Does this support premium/discount multiple? By how much?
- **Growth**: What does this imply for sustainable growth rate?
- **Risk**: What downside scenarios does this create? Probability and magnitude?
- **Catalyst timeline**: When might competitive dynamics change?

### Uncertainty Acknowledgment
Where data is limited or analysis is subjective:
- Clearly state assumptions
- Provide range of outcomes vs. point estimates
- Flag low-confidence assessments
- Suggest additional diligence areas

## Integration: Competitive Strength Scorecard

After completing all 11 sections, create overall competitive strength score:

**Scoring methodology** (1-10 scale for each dimension):
1. Market Structure & Positioning: Market share rank and trend
2. Competitive Moat: Width and durability of moat
3. Porter's Five Forces: Industry attractiveness
4. Value Chain & Cost Position: Cost leadership or differentiation strength
5. Strategic Positioning: Strategy clarity and execution
6. Innovation & Adaptability: R&D efficiency and track record
7. Operational Competitiveness: Asset efficiency and quality
8. Financial Competitiveness: ROIC spread and margin sustainability
9. Vulnerability Analysis: Risk severity (inverted - fewer vulnerabilities = higher score)
10. Forward-Looking Indicators: Trajectory positive/neutral/negative
11. Geopolitical Supply Chain Resilience: Supply chain security and friend-shored advantage

**Overall score**: Weighted average with adjustable weights based on industry
- Default: Equal weight (~9% each)
- Capital-intensive: Overweight operations, financial metrics, supply chain resilience
- Tech/innovation-driven: Overweight moat, innovation, forward indicators
- Mature/stable: Overweight market position, value chain, financial returns
- Hardware/manufacturing: Overweight supply chain resilience (15-20%), operations, value chain
- Software/services: Underweight supply chain resilience (5%), overweight moat, innovation

**2026 geopolitical environment adjustment:**
In sustained high-geopolitical-risk environments, consider upweighting Section 11 (Geopolitical Supply Chain Resilience) by 1.5-2x for:
- Companies with >30% revenue from hardware/physical goods
- Industries with critical material dependencies (semis, batteries, pharma, defense)
- Companies with significant China/Taiwan manufacturing exposure

**Interpretation guide:**
- 8.0-10.0: Dominant competitor, wide moat, highly attractive
- 6.5-7.9: Strong position, sustainable advantages, attractive
- 5.0-6.4: Adequate position, modest advantages, fair
- 3.0-4.9: Weak position, vulnerable, unattractive
- 1.0-2.9: Severely disadvantaged, existential risk

**Output**: 
- Scorecard table with dimension scores
- Radar chart visualization
- Written interpretation with investment recommendation
