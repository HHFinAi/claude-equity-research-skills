## 1. SaaS / Cloud Software

### Must-Extract KPIs
| KPI | Where to Find | Why It Matters |
|-----|--------------|----------------|
| ARR / Total ARR | Earnings release, supplement | Core growth metric; watch for calculation changes |
| Net Revenue Retention (NRR/DBNR) | Earnings release, call | >120% excellent, <100% contraction; most important SaaS metric |
| RPO (Remaining Performance Obligations) | 10-Q Note on Revenue | Forward revenue visibility; decompose current vs non-current |
| Customer count (total + $100K+ cohort) | Earnings release | Land-and-expand health; large customer concentration risk |
| Gross margin (subscription vs services) | Income statement, supplement | Subscription >75% is healthy; services drag is normal but watch trajectory |
| Rule of 40 (Revenue growth % + FCF margin %) | Calculated | >40 = healthy; decompose which leg is carrying |
| CAC Payback / S&M as % of revenue | Calculated from financials | Efficiency improving or deteriorating as scale grows? |
| Billings growth vs revenue growth | Cash flow + deferred revenue | Divergence signals acceleration or deceleration before it hits revenue |

### Red Flags Specific to SaaS
- NRR declining for 3+ consecutive quarters
- RPO growth decelerating while revenue growth maintained (backlog drawdown)
- Rising services mix (lower quality revenue)
- Deferred revenue declining QoQ in a seasonally neutral quarter
- New non-GAAP adjustments excluding restructuring from "adjusted" metrics

### Management Language to Monitor
- Shift from "land and expand" to "consolidation" or "platform" framing
- Commentary on macro impact on deal cycles (elongated sales cycles, budget scrutiny)
- AI-related language: "co-pilot", "embedded AI", "AI-native" — watch for substance vs buzzwords
- Changes in how they define or calculate NRR

## 2. Semiconductors

### Must-Extract KPIs
| KPI | Where to Find | Why It Matters |
|-----|--------------|----------------|
| Revenue by end market | Supplement, call | Data center/AI vs consumer vs auto vs industrial mix shift |
| ASP trends | MD&A, call | Pricing power indicator; decompose from volume |
| Gross margin | Income statement | Cycle indicator: peak margins = peak cycle risk |
| Utilisation rates | Call, supplement (for fabs) | Below 80% = margin pressure; above 90% = capacity constrained |
| Inventory (own + channel) | Balance sheet, call | Days of inventory >120 in non-memory = demand risk |
| Design wins / pipeline | Call, prepared remarks | Forward demand indicator; quality > quantity |
| Capex and depreciation | Cash flow, call | Capex cycle timing; over-investment risk at cycle peak |
| HBM / AI-specific revenue | Call, supplement | The metric that matters most in current cycle |
| Book-to-bill ratio | Supplement, call | >1.0 = order growth exceeding shipments |

### Cycle Positioning Framework
- **Early cycle**: Revenue bottoming, inventory normalising, utilisation troughing, orders recovering
- **Mid cycle**: Revenue accelerating, margins expanding, capex ramping, customer urgency rising
- **Late cycle**: Revenue growth peaking, margins at highs, inventory building, lead times extending
- **Downturn**: Revenue declining, inventory correction, utilisation falling, capex cuts

### Red Flags Specific to Semis
- Inventory build at customer and company level simultaneously
- Gross margin expansion driven by underloading charges (unsustainable)
- Capex significantly exceeding depreciation for 3+ quarters without revenue acceleration
- Double-ordering signals (lead time extension → customers over-order → correction)
- China revenue % spiking before export control deadlines (pull-forward)

## 3. Biotech / Pharma

### Must-Extract KPIs
| KPI | Where to Find | Why It Matters |
|-----|--------------|----------------|
| Product revenue by drug | Earnings release, supplement | Launch curve tracking; franchise health |
| Rx volume (TRx/NBRx) | Call, IQVIA data | Volume vs pricing decomposition |
| Pipeline milestones (Phase, readout timing) | Call, 10-Q risk factors | Binary catalyst calendar; probability-adjusted value |
| R&D expense and productivity | Income statement, pipeline | $ per IND, success rates, prioritisation signals |
| Cash runway | Balance sheet | For pre-revenue: months of cash at current burn |
| Patent / LOE timeline | 10-K, call | Revenue cliff risk; biosimilar entry timing |
| Collaboration / licensing income | Revenue detail | Quality: milestone-based or recurring royalties? |
| Inventory in channel | Call, supplement | Channel stocking vs destocking; launch dynamics |

### Red Flags Specific to Biotech
- Cash runway <18 months without clear financing path
- Pivoting lead indication or changing primary endpoint mid-trial
- Management selling shares during quiet period before data readout
- CRL (Complete Response Letter) or FDA delay without clear resubmission timeline
- Declining TRx despite increasing promotional spend (launch failure)

## 4. Banks / Financials

### Must-Extract KPIs
| KPI | Where to Find | Why It Matters |
|-----|--------------|----------------|
| NII and NIM | Income statement, supplement | Core earnings power; sensitivity to rate curve |
| Loan growth (by category) | Supplement, call | CRE vs C&I vs consumer mix; growth quality |
| Deposit costs and mix | Supplement, call | Beta on deposits; non-interest bearing % declining = NIM pressure |
| Credit quality: NCOs, NPLs, reserves | Supplement | NCO rate trend; reserve build/release; reserve-to-NPL coverage |
| CET1 ratio | Supplement | Capital adequacy; buyback capacity; distance from regulatory minimum |
| Fee income mix | Income statement detail | Wealth management, trading, advisory — diversification and cyclicality |
| Provision for credit losses | Income statement | Forward-looking credit view; reserve build = bearish signal |
| ROTCE | Calculated | >15% excellent; <10% below cost of equity for most banks |
| Efficiency ratio | Calculated | <55% strong; >65% weak; trend matters more than level |

### Red Flags Specific to Banks
- Reserve release driving EPS beat (unsustainable; eventually reverses)
- CRE (commercial real estate) concentration >300% of capital
- NIM compression accelerating faster than deposit repricing
- Non-performing loan migration from watch to substandard categories
- Trading revenue volatility masking weak core banking trends

## 5. Consumer / Retail

### Must-Extract KPIs
| KPI | Where to Find | Why It Matters |
|-----|--------------|----------------|
| Same-store sales / Comps | Earnings release | The single most important retail metric |
| Traffic vs ticket decomposition | Call, supplement | Traffic = demand health; ticket = pricing power / trade-up/down |
| E-commerce mix and growth | Earnings release, call | Channel shift dynamics; margin implications |
| Inventory per store / Inventory turns | Balance sheet, calculated | Rising inventory = markdown risk |
| Gross margin | Income statement | Promotional intensity; input cost pass-through |
| Store count changes (openings, closures) | Supplement, call | Growth via units vs comps; saturation risk |
| Consumer sentiment indicators | Call, management commentary | Management read on consumer health; trade-down signals |

### Red Flags Specific to Consumer
- Positive comps driven entirely by ticket (price) with negative traffic
- Inventory growth > revenue growth for 2+ quarters
- Rising promotional activity / markdown rates
- E-commerce growth decelerating toward physical store growth rates
- Management shifting from unit growth to "optimising the fleet"

## 6. Industrials / Capital Goods

### Must-Extract KPIs
| KPI | Where to Find | Why It Matters |
|-----|--------------|----------------|
| Organic orders and order growth | Supplement, call | Leading indicator; strip out large one-time orders |
| Book-to-bill ratio | Supplement, calculated | >1.0 = demand exceeding supply; <1.0 = backlog drawdown |
| Backlog / RPO | Supplement, call | Revenue visibility; quality matters (cancellable vs firm) |
| Price vs volume decomposition | Call, MD&A | Pricing power vs underlying demand |
| Segment margin bridge | Supplement, call | Volume leverage, price/cost spread, mix, productivity |
| Restructuring / transformation costs | Income statement, non-GAAP bridge | Are "one-time" charges becoming recurring? |
| Free cash flow conversion (FCF / Net Income) | Calculated | Industrials should run >90% conversion |

### Red Flags Specific to Industrials
- Orders declining while backlog still elevated (peak backlog = peak cycle)
- Margin expansion driven entirely by price while volume flat/declining
- Working capital consuming cash despite revenue growth
- "Restructuring" charges appearing in 3+ consecutive years
- Short-cycle orders declining while long-cycle masks the weakness

## 7. REITs / Real Estate

### Must-Extract KPIs
| KPI | Where to Find | Why It Matters |
|-----|--------------|----------------|
| FFO / AFFO per share | Earnings release | REIT equivalent of EPS; FFO = GAAP net income + depreciation - gains |
| Occupancy rate | Supplement | Physical vs economic occupancy; watch the gap |
| Lease spreads (cash and GAAP) | Supplement, call | Pricing power on renewals; cash vs GAAP spread matters |
| Same-property NOI growth | Supplement | Organic portfolio performance excluding acquisitions |
| Development pipeline | Supplement, call | Growth visibility; yields on cost vs cap rates |
| Debt maturity profile | 10-Q, supplement | Refinancing risk; weighted average interest rate trend |
| NAV per share | Analyst estimates, calculated | Premium or discount to NAV; catalysts to close gap |
| Dividend payout ratio (vs AFFO) | Calculated | >90% = limited growth investment capacity; <70% = buffer |

## 8. Energy / Oil & Gas

### Must-Extract KPIs
| KPI | Where to Find | Why It Matters |
|-----|--------------|----------------|
| Production volumes (BOE/d) | Earnings release, supplement | Growth trajectory; organic vs acquired |
| Realised prices vs benchmark | Supplement, call | Differential management; hedging impact |
| Cash operating costs per BOE | Calculated from supplement | All-in cost structure; breakeven price |
| Reserves replacement ratio | Annual filing (10-K) | Sustainability of production base |
| FCF and shareholder return framework | Call, supplement | Buyback + dividend as % of FCF; capital discipline |
| Capex guidance and reinvestment rate | Call, supplement | Growth capex vs maintenance; discipline vs growth |
| Breakeven price | Calculated or call | Margin of safety at current commodity prices |

## 9. Media / Advertising

### Must-Extract KPIs
| KPI | Where to Find | Why It Matters |
|-----|--------------|----------------|
| Subscriber count and net adds | Earnings release | Growth trajectory; churn rate; ARPU trends |
| ARPU (Average Revenue Per User) | Calculated or supplement | Monetisation trajectory; pricing power |
| Ad revenue growth and pricing (CPM trends) | Earnings release, call | Ad market health; share gains vs market growth |
| Content spend and amortisation | Cash flow, income statement | Cash content spend vs P&L; growing or rationalising? |
| Engagement metrics (DAU, time spent, impressions) | Supplement, call | Usage driving monetisation? Or engagement declining? |
| Churn rate | Supplement, call (if disclosed) | Gross vs net; voluntary vs involuntary |

## Using This Checklist

1. Identify the company's sector from the list above
2. Extract ALL must-extract KPIs for that sector in addition to the universal KPIs in the main SKILL.md
3. Check for sector-specific red flags
4. If a company spans sectors (e.g., Amazon = cloud + retail + advertising), apply multiple checklists
5. For sectors not listed, adapt the closest match and note the limitation
